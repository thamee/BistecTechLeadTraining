# 1:1 Meeting Templates and Notes

## Standard 1:1 Template

### Pre-Meeting Prep

- [ ] Review previous 1:1 notes
- [ ] Check any pending action items
- [ ] Note any observations to discuss
- [ ] Review project progress

### Meeting Structure (30 min)

1. **Opening (2 min)**
   - How are you doing?
   - Anything on your mind?
2. **Their Agenda (15 min)**
   - What would you like to discuss?
   - [Space for their topics]
3. **My Topics (8 min)**
   - [Specific feedback]
   - [Updates they need to know]
4. **Close (5 min)**
   - Action items recap
   - Anything else?
   - Next meeting date

### Post-Meeting

- [ ] Document key points
- [ ] Send action items
- [ ] Schedule any follow-ups

## Customized Templates by Team Member

### Alex (Aspiring Tech Lead)

#### Opening
Start by checking in on Alex’s overall well-being and workload to create a comfortable and open environment.
- How has your week been?
- How are you feeling about your current workload?

Since Alex is a high performer, also briefly touch on the overall project:
- How do you feel about the current project status?
- Any concerns or risks you’re seeing in the team or project?

#### Discussion Focus

**1. Project & Ownership**
- Discuss current progress and any upcoming milestones
- Share updates on project plans or direction
- Ask if he needs any support from management

**2. Recognition & Expectations**
- Acknowledge key achievements and contributions
- Reinforce expectations around ownership and accountability
- Encourage him to take more initiative in leading work

**3. Growth Towards Tech Lead**
- Talk about opportunities to lead parts of projects
- Involve him in technical decisions and planning
- Encourage mentoring 
**4. Ideas & Feedback**
- Ask about any initiatives or improvements he would like to introduce
- Gather feedback on current team practices and processes

#### Sample Questions
- How do you feel about the direction of the current project?
- Is there anything you’d like to take more ownership of?
- What kind of leadership opportunities would you like to explore?
- Do you feel you’re getting enough visibility across the team?
- Do you have any ideas to improve how we’re currently working?

---

#### Action Items
- Help Alex transition from individual contributor to leader
- Build confidence in ownership and decision-making
- Prepare him for a future Tech Lead role
 


### Jordan (Mid-Level – Developing)

#### Opening
Start by checking in on Jordan’s current workload and any challenges he wants to discuss:  
- How has your week been?  
- Are there any tasks or areas you’re finding difficult right now?  

Acknowledge his effort so far:  
- Appreciate the work you’ve done and the progress you’ve made  

---

#### Discussion Focus

**1. Challenges & Support**
- Let Jordan share the challenges he’s facing  
- Offer advice or ideas on how to approach difficult tasks  
- Encourage him to ask for help or collaborate with teammates when needed  

**2. Skill Development**
- Share constructive suggestions for improvement over the next month  
- Recommend learning materials, courses, or training opportunities  
- Discuss how these will help him handle more complex tasks  

**3. Future Contributions**
- Talk about upcoming projects or initiatives he could contribute to  
- Encourage him to take small ownership steps in tasks  
- Highlight opportunities to learn from other team members  

---

#### Sample Questions
- Which tasks have been most challenging for you recently?  
- Where do you feel stuck or need guidance?  
- Are there specific skills you’d like to improve?  
- How can I or the team support you better?  
- Is there any initiative you’d like to contribute to in the coming weeks?  

---

#### Action
- Help Jordan gain confidence in tackling complex tasks  
- Provide actionable guidance and support  
- Encourage proactive learning and collaboration  
- Build a clear path for improvement and growth


#### Follow ups
Monthly Sync up on his progress 

### Riley (Underperforming - Personal Issues)

#### Opening
Start by checking in on Riley’s well-being and making him feel comfortable:  
- How are you doing personally and at work?  
- Anything on your mind this week?  

Acknowledge past contributions to build confidence:  
- Highlight specific accomplishments and strengths  
- Reinforce that he has the potential to grow and succeed  

---

#### Discussion Focus

**1. Understanding & Support**
- Discuss any personal challenges affecting work in a supportive way  
- Ask what level of workload feels manageable right now  
- Reassure him that management and the team are there to support him  

**2. Skill Development & Guidance**
- Suggest learning materials or training opportunities that could help improve focus and confidence  
- Give small, achievable goals to rebuild momentum  

**3. Future Planning**
- Encourage him to identify areas where he can contribute meaningfully  
- Discuss possible adjustments to workload or responsibilities temporarily  
- Emphasize continuous support and open communication  

---

#### Sample Questions
- How are you feeling about work and your current tasks?  
- Are there any obstacles that are making things difficult right now?  
- What would make your work feel more manageable?  
- Are there skills or knowledge areas you’d like to improve?  
- How can I or the team best support you during this time?  

---

#### Action
- Provide empathy and understanding first  
- Stabilize performance while addressing personal challenges  
- Rebuild confidence and focus  
- Set a clear path for gradual improvement and support

#### Follow Ups 
Montly Sync Up
